The smooth curves in the chart above are fits of the MacCoull–Walther viscosity–temperature equation to each oil’s reported values. This equation is described in ASTM D-341. For comparison, the chart also shows #2 diesel fuel: the bold black curve is the correlation of Tat & Van Gerpen (1999), and the red dashed line is the ASTM D975 kinematic-viscosity limit for #2 diesel (1.9–4.1 mm²/s at 40 °C; the line marks the 4.1 maximum). Most vegetable oils exceed the diesel limit until heated.
